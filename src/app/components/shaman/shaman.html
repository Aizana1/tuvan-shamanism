The Mystery of Tuvan Shamans

Based on Chapter III of "Tuvan Shamans" by M.B. Kenin-Lopsan

1. Forms of Kamlanie

Field materials collected in all districts of Tuva reveal several main types of Kamlanie (rituals). These vary depending on local conditions, the shaman's power, and their artistic skill.

Kuzungu-lug Khamnaary

(Kamlanie with a Mirror)

Every crowned shaman must have a Kuzungu (bronze mirror). With it, one can perform healing rituals. Historically, ancient found bronze mirrors were highly valued for divination and rituals even without full attire.

Source: Inst bubenshamana

Khomustap Khamnaary

(Kamlanie with a Khomus)

Common in Erzin and Tes-Khem. Shamans performed rituals artistically, accompanying themselves on a Sheler-Khomus (musical instrument). Typically characteristic of "weaker" shamans.

Source: https://kntuva.ru/post/1/1047/

Derig-Dungurlug Khamnaary

(Kamlanie with Drum & Cloak)

High-class professional shamans were required to have a full set: a drum (*Dungur*) and ritual cloak (*Derig*). A "true strong shaman" never shamanized without these attributes.

Source: National Museum of the Republic of Tuva

Dayaktyg Khamnaary

(Kamlanie with a Staff)

According to S.I. Vainshtein, the use of a birch staff called Dayak was characteristic of shamans in the Todzha district.

Source: National Museum of the Republic of Tuva

2. The Procedure of Kamlanie

A Tuvan shaman can perform rituals at any time, but late evening and night are considered most suitable. "The shaman destined to shamanize, leads the ritual in the black night."

Calling the Shaman (Khamny Khooru)

The patient chooses the shaman. A messenger is sent with a spare horse. If the shaman agrees, he attaches his gear to the saddle and hides the Kuzungu (mirror) in his bosom, allowing no one to touch it.

Lighting the Censer (Chyttyg San Salyry)

Before any ritual, the shaman must light the San (censer). Burning coals and juniper (*artysh*) are placed on a flat stone. The shaman purifies his drum and boots over the smoke, standing on one leg. This gives him strength for the battle with evil spirits.

Source: Управление Верховного Шамана VK

Beginning of Kamlanie (Khamnap Egelaeri)

The shaman beats the drum and circles the censer three times (following the sun). He cries like a cuckoo or raven, signaling the start, and begins a silent conversation with his spirit helpers (*Eerens*).

Creating the Image of Disease (Aarygyn Chuuluzun Kolyry)

The shaman orders the creation of an "image of the disease." He cuts figures of evil spirits from paper and attaches colorful fabric scraps. This Chuulu is attached to a board (*daspan*) to be carried out later.

Making Ogaalga (Ogaalga Kylyry)

A small square of felt (*Ogaalga*) is prepared with tea leaves and raw meat. This is food to lure the disease spirits out.

Preparation for Removal (Seriir Choogunda)

Towards the end, the censer and *Ogaalga* are carried out. The carrier circles the patient three times with the smoking censer, luring the spirits with the pleasant smell and the taste of meat.

Holy Water (Arzhaan Kylyry)

Before the items are removed, the patient washes with "holy water" (*Arzhaan*): water mixed with milk and juniper. Drops must fall onto the disease image (*Chuulu*), not on the ground.

Divination (Toorek Kaary)

The shaman throws his beater (*Orba*) to predict the future:

  • Anaa Toorek (Good Omen): Fur side up. "My shaman, the divination is worth silver and gold!"
  • Uygu Toorek (Sleep/Bad Omen): Wood side up. The shaman must drum again to avert danger.
  • Kyrlan Toorek (Neutral): Falls on the side. The shaman flips it and throws again.

Payment (Asty)

Rich patients gave cattle or a horse with silver harness. Poor patients gave a felt rug, skins, or personal ornaments like rings or buttons.

Offering to Attributes (Khamnyn Et-Sevinge)

Symbolic gifts for the shaman's tools: a small bag (*eldik*) with a leather cord, needle, thimble, and tobacco. The attributes must be honored.
